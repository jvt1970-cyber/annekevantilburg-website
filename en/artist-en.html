Her Art

Anneke as an Artist

Her Story

From "Not Creative" to
the Power of Expression

For a long time, I lived with the narrative that I was not creative. At school, I was told I couldn't draw and that crafts were not for me. Looking back, I understand why: I have a deep resistance to having to copy. For me, creativity is not about replication — it is about what lives within.

It was only much later, during a period of recovery after whiplash and PTSD, that I discovered the true power of the brush. Painting was not a hobby then — it was pure necessity. It was the only way to get out of my head and back into my body. It was processing, creation as a path to healing.

Working method

Freedom in Cutting
and Connection

My artistic journey is one of continuous development. For years, my process was based on the surprise of the cut. I worked intuitively on large surfaces, then cut the work into small pieces of 10×10 or 10×15 cm. That method gave me the ultimate freedom; it didn't have to work as a whole. It was only when turning the fragments over that I discovered the hidden gems.

In September 2025, a large collage work — my AVT canvas — marked the completion of this phase. Since then, my work is also allowed to remain intact. I have discovered that a passe-partout and a frame not only finish a work, but also give it the space to be truly seen. It is a form of taking myself more seriously as an artist.

"Removing layers to reveal depth — that is where my art and my coaching come together."

Art & Coaching

Where Painting and
Coaching Meet

My current work is an exploration of depth through Mixed Media. I apply countless layers of paint, only to sand them back down. As I sand, the underlying colours and textures re-emerge.

This process is a direct reflection of the deep dive I make in my coaching practice. In life, we often mask our vulnerable sides. By sanding — by asking deeper questions, polishing, and looking further — we discover the old layers we once covered up. When we dare to expose those layers at the right moment, the true beauty and essence emerge.

Beyond the Studio

More than Just Art

Beyond my studio, I love being in nature or behind the decks. I love movement, laughter, and deep conversations that actually go somewhere. I am constantly searching for light, joy, and co-creation.

I invite you to wander through my work. Perhaps in the layers of my paintings, you will recognise something of your own story.
